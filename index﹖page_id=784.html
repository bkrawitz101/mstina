Thank you, Kelly, Gabriela, and Jonathan!
I deeply appreciate all of your encouraging compliments, comments, and suggestions, which helped me understand there may be some misunderstanding to these videos that appeared like animated cartoons. Since there seems to be a general consensus on that point, I will make a general response to that point and detail more on how these VR classrooms work. 


They are live and completely immersive VR environments. Each student participates in a first-person view as though they are in front of an instructor in physical reality. The ‘video’ was shot in this way to demonstrate how an actual class will operate. These videos are not cartoon animations for students to watch, they are 3D graphic representations of classroom environments, teaching materials, and of the people in them, live and in real-time.  So in essence, the students do not watch a recorded video but participate in a live 3D VR environment, which is available on VR goggles, like Oculus Quest as well as in 2D on a standard computer, both Windows and Mac.  


My husband is developing applications that will allow the instructor to instantaneously produce ‘in-world’ any 3D object that is contained in our library of teaching objects. This app will insert words and objects that match them, for example, the word ‘Apple’ right next to a 3D apple, which the teacher and students will be able to interact with, including moving them around as they would in a video game. He is also planning on creating an entire set of Cuisenaire rods, with the words already printed on them. It will also enable the students to have pens to write within the VR classroom. Tilt Brush is an example of this. 


VR has given us a whole new level of possibilities for remote teaching, including being able to teach students globally in fantastical worlds of imagination, where anything could be made possible, fully immersive and interactive without losing social contact, rather than just the one-way video conference type of remote learning. Any teacher will be able to come into our classrooms with their goggles or computer and teach their students with the material we provide or custom make for their class. I hope you will all join us and please feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn or Facebook, so I can give you a virtual tour of these classrooms to experience and know them for yourself.
