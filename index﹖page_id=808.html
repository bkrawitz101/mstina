Professional Goals

When I began the TESOL program at UCLA Extension, it coincided with my discovery of the AltspaceVR Virtual Reality social media platform as a potential host for the VR classroom environments I was planning to create and eventually teach in.  My goals at the start were simply to learn how to teach ESL, improve my knowledge of the English grammar, to create VR classrooms, and to teach in them. 


Through the last six months of the program during the pandemic that continued relentlessly, I witnessed a strong market need to expand those goals to include founding a global startup, at a minimum, that creates and sells VR classrooms and their usage to schools, teachers, and students.  


During the Technology course last semester, I saw a website building template for a school and it gave me the idea of propelling that startup idea to becoming a school.  It would have to depend on getting the funding of course, but that new vision quickly developed into my four new professional goals for the next three years:


  1.          • To form a truly international and successful online school offering VR classrooms exclusively and includes teaching materials.


  1.          • To bring together a community of institutions, educators, learners, and content creators from around the world, to co-create and enjoy                         educational experiences in these fully immersive and Interactive classroom environments in real-time.


  1.          •To expand the program offerings in my school to include not only English classes, but also other language classes and eventually other           subjects as well.


  1.         •To have my school be represented and operate globally.  With the capital earned, explore other VR platforms beyond AltspaceVR, currently a          free platform for content creators and users.  These will include Engage, a VR platform dedicated to education that is accessible in Mainland          China on a locally licensed version, which will overcome the challenge of accessibility AltspaceVR has, requiring the use of a VPN to reach the          World-Wide-Web beyond their national firewall of content control.
